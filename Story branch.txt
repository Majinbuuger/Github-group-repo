Helen Platt was thinking about Robert Superhalk again. Robert was a spiteful juggler with pointy arms and handsome eyebrows.
Helen walked over to the window and reflected on her beautiful surroundings. She had always loved derelict West Boggins with its distinct, damaged ditches. It was a place that encouraged her tendency to feel concerned.
Then she saw something in the distance, or rather someone. It was the a spiteful figure of Robert Superhalk.
Helen gulped. She glanced at her own reflection. She was a charming, daring, wine drinker with slimy arms and sloppy eyebrows. Her friends

But not even a charming person who had once helped a happy blind person recover from a flying accident, was prepared for what Robert had in store today.
The wind blew like swimming ostriches, making Helen afraid. Helen grabbed a tattered gun that had been strewn nearby; she massaged it with her fingers.
As Helen stepped outside and Robert came closer, she could see the petite smile on his face.
